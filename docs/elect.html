Total Letters Narrative

To address potential confounding in across-legislator comparisons, the estimates from Model 2 (Column 2 of Table \ref{tab:models_total}) provide the estimated effects from the difference-in-differences specification in Equation \ref{e:diff1}. Consider first the effect of being in a competitive district. We estimate that a district becoming competitive causes an increase of -0.04 requests \textit{per agency} (95-percent confidence interval [-0.13, 0.05]). Across all 92 agencies, this represents an increase of approximately -3 additional requests per year, -2.8\% of the average requests per year in our data. There is a smaller increase for individuals who become ranking members and those who join a Prestige Committee, though the increase is statistically significant for the prestige committee. Becoming a ranking member of a committee causes an increase of 0.16 contacts per agency, while joining a prestige committee causes a -0.06 per agency increase in the number of contacts a member of Congress makes.

We estimate that the experience gained between the first and second year in Congress causes an increase of 0.18 requests \textit{per agency}. The experience gained between the first and seventh year causes an increase of 0.26 per agency. Across all n_agency agencies, this represents an increase of approximately 24 additional requests per year, 19.4\% of the average requests per year in our data. There is a smaller increase after the second year. The experience gained between the second and seventh year causes an increase of 0.09 per agency, an increase of approximately 8 additional requests per year, 6.4\% of the average requests per year in our data.
